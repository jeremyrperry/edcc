Reflections

Having time to reflect upon taking CIS 241 this quarter, there are a few things that have come to mind.

First off, I learned that developing and maintaining a web site is a lot of work. I have personally spent hours developing this site, and yet it only has some of the most basic elements to make it functional. I know that if I am to continue in the field of web development, I have my work cut out for me. Fortunately, I enjoy working with the web and technology in general, so I'm looking forward to it.

I also had a couple ah-ha moments this quarter. The first was when, as a Mac user, I noticed that all of the instructions for FTPing and text editor options were listed for PC users. I knew that I had to do something about this. Instead of twiddling my thumbs, I hit the ground running and was able to find a very useable freeware text editor and several different options. Because I knew I'm not completely alone in being a Mac user, I posted the options in the discussion board. My instructor liked them enough to where she wanted to post them as permanent instructions, and I let her. Long story short, I learned that I will often have to think above and beyond the simple instructions to figure out what will work for me. When I did, I got a lot of satisfaction knowing that what I found will continue to help others.

My second ah-ha moment as during the CSS-table debate discussion. I learned from it that while XHTML does have a basic science to it, it also has an art form as well. I came to the conclusion that in order to be a good web developer, I need to know as much as I can about the different ways to create a web page. That will come in handy for when I actually take my skills to the real world and can advise a client or development team on which approach will work best for them.

Overall, CIS 241 has been a great class to take and I look forward to developing my skills as a web developer even more.
