Welcome to my CIS 241 website!

I created this website as a demonstration of the skills I learned in CIS 241, which is an introduction to XHTML. Everything on this site has been created from scratch using XHTML standards as determined by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). The image to the left contains links to various pages within my website. Below is a brief description of each:

  • My Resume - An up-to-date XHTML version of my resume. It is in a plain-text format for ease of scanning.
  • My Guestbook - A form I created for people who have visited my site to leave me a comment. You can use it to leave me feedback on my site.
  • My Interests - A little bit about me and what I like to do when I'm not busy with school.
  • Reflections - Here you can see some of my thoughts about taking CIS 241.
  • My "Cases" Page - This is a springboard page to some of the XHTML skillsets that I learned this term.

About Me

My name is Jeremy Perry. I am currently working on a Web Application Developer degree at Edmonds Community College, hence why I am taking this class. I already have a Bachelor's degree in English (Creative Writing Emphasis) with a minor in Journalism from Western Washington University, but since the economy is tough, I decided to keep doing some form of education. Since the internet and computers in general have always been a passion of mine, I decided to go the web development route. I am expecting to be finished with the program at the end of Summer quarter of 2011.

One of the things I like to do is travelling. In fact, since this class and the other class I took during Summer 2010 were online, I was able to travel for a little over a month through Europe for the first time. Here are the places I visited during my trip:

  • Ireland - Dublin and Galway
  • Spain - Barcelona
  • France - Paris
  • Germany - Hamburg
  • The Netherlands - Amsterdam
  • United Kingdom - London

The picture above was an interesting shot of me trying not to be hit by taffic on the Pont d'lena bridge to get a night picture with the Eiffel Tower in the background.

While they technically don't count as places visited, I had brief layovers in Frankfort, Germany and Zurich, Switzerland. Even though I had the time of my life, my grades didn't suffer one bit as I earned A's in both of my classes. I thoroughly celebrated that accomplishment in Amsterdam ;) How's that for studying abroad?

Travelling aside, I also like to fish, hunt, ski, ride bicycles, work out, sail and a whole multitude of other outdoor activities. I am currently 29 years old and a Navy veteran (I visited a lot of Asia during my service). I love good rock n' roll music, though I'm not opposed to hearing other music. Maybe one day I might find out the meaning of life and the love of my life, but until then I will at least live life up as much as I can.
